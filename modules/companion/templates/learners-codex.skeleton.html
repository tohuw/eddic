01 Your turn, in order

Five separate budgets, every turn. Interleave them freely.

  1. MoveUp to 30 ft; split it around your action.
  2. ActionYour big thing — menu in card 02.
  3. Bonus actionOnly when something grants one — for you, Cunning Action.
  4. Object interactionOne free quick thing: draw a dagger, yank a lever.
  5. ReactionOne per round, usually off-turn — see the reactions box.

You can also talk, and you can pass.

02 The action menu

What your action can be. Never just "attack."

anyone everyone at the table can do this yours a class thing, unique to you
  • Attackanyone — a weapon or unarmed strike.
  • Dashanyone — double your movement.
  • Disengageanyone — move without provoking.
  • Dodgeanyone — attackers roll at disadvantage on you.
  • Helpanyone — hand an ally advantage.
  • Hideanyone — Stealth vs. Perception.
  • Readyanyone — "when X, I do Y." Spends your reaction.
  • Search / Study / Influenceanyone — look, recall, sway.
  • Shove / Grappleanyone — knock prone or hold.
  • Improviseanyone — anything you can describe.

Almost the whole menu is universal — your tablemates pick from the same list. When someone's turn stalls, any "anyone" action is on the table for them too.

Reactions — your off-turn move

The whole idea, since beginners forget they have one.

  • Everyone gets one per round; it refreshes at the start of your turn.
  • It fires on someone else's turn, in response to a trigger.

Your reaction options:

  • Opportunity attack — a free strike when a foe leaves your reach. No setup.
  • Ready — the one you do pre-plan, on your turn.
The catch

One reaction between your turns. A Ready spends the same reaction you'd need for an opportunity attack — not both.

⚡ Things you probably didn't know — or haven't considered

The stuff that turns "I attack" into a real turn

Not advice on what to pick — just rules new players miss, so your options are wider than they look.

You have a reaction every round

A whole extra action, off-turn. See the reactions box.

Leaving reach provokes — but not always

Moving within reach, forced movement, and teleport don't. Disengage cancels it.

Split your move around your action

Move, strike, move the rest. It's one turn.

Help is a gift you can give

Trade your action for an ally's advantage — and, for a rogue, it sets up an ally's flank too.

Advantage doesn't stack

It's 2d20 take the better, no matter how many reasons.

Zero HP isn't death

Death saves; a nat 20 revives at 1; allies can stabilize you.
