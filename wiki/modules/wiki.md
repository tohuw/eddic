# wiki

The wiki module gives a campaign its knowledge architecture: the source layer, the DM-master wiki, page-level visibility, twin pages for progressive reveal, the typed operation log, and the deterministic player projection. It is the substrate every player-facing surface builds on, and the schema a maintaining agent keeps across sessions. It depends on [cli](cli.md) and [lint](lint.md), and its projection output is what downstream surfaces such as [render](render.md) and [publish](publish.md) consume. The module is entirely local and free: markdown files plus a stdlib projection with no paid dependencies.

## Three layers

A campaign under the wiki schema separates raw material, curated master, and generated projection. The `sources/` layer holds the unprocessed input — the owner's and players' own writing, shared documents, and session transcripts — each file carrying `authorship:` frontmatter. The `wiki/` layer is the single maintained master: facts compiled from sources, interlinked in encyclopedia tone, holding the full table truth including secrets. The `dist/player/` layer is the player projection, generated by the projection verb and never edited by hand; every player-facing surface reads only from it. Instruction files (`AGENTS.md`, `CLAUDE.md`, `README.md`) are treated as non-content and never published.

## Visibility and twin pages

Every wiki page carries `visibility:` frontmatter and fails closed: a page with no marker is DM-only, and only `visibility: player` projects. Where a topic reveals progressively, it is kept as twin pages — a player page under the canonical name holding exactly what the table knows now, and a DM page at the same location with a `.dm.md` suffix holding the full truth. The DM twin may link its player twin; the player twin never links back, because a player page may only link other player pages. This constraint is what makes the firewall mechanically enforceable rather than a matter of vigilance. Assets under `wiki/assets/` project wholesale, so spoiler assets are never placed there; DM assets live under a path containing `.dm`, which never projects. See [Projection and visibility](../concepts/projection-and-visibility.md) and [The firewall](../concepts/the-firewall.md) for the underlying model.

## The projection verb

The module vendors a `project` verb into the campaign's `.eddic/lib/project.py`. It copies pages marked `visibility: player` from the master into the projection directory, preserving the tree, and applies contributor overlays first. Its defining property is that no agent judgment enters the file anywhere: the firewall is checked before a single byte is written, and any breach refuses the whole projection all-or-nothing. A breach is a player-visible page linking a page that is not player-visible, does not exist, or escapes the wiki root — a link that would either leak or lie once in players' hands. Contributor material lives under `contribs/<id>/`; a contrib file carrying `replaces:` shadows a base page on every built surface while the base stays untouched in the tree, so removing a contributor is deterministic. Two contribs claiming one target, or a contrib landing on a base page without declaring `replaces:`, refuses projection rather than guessing. Exit codes are 0 for projected, 1 for refused with breaches listed, 2 for a usage error.

## Attribution and the operation log

Authorship is captured at write time or lost: each source file records a short pseudonymous `authorship:` contributor id, with the id-to-identity roster kept DM-only so real names never enter the wiki. Human-authored prose is never stylistically rewritten — only owner-directed mechanical transforms are permitted, and they are logged. The operation log, named in config and appended to in the wiki directory, records every significant action as `## [YYYY-MM-DD] <type> | <summary>` with the type drawn from a fixed set: `ingest`, `reconcile`, `lint`, `schema`, `witness`, `attribution`, `consent`, and `sever`. Entries are append-only and never rewritten, and relative dates are converted to absolute. Contributions, derivation links, and the author's clean-room severance are tracked through this log and the [contribs](contribs.md) module's overlay mechanics.

## Applying the pattern

The pattern stamps the schema and seeds — the campaign `AGENTS.md`, a `CLAUDE.md` stub, and the `index.md`, `index.dm.md`, and log seeds if absent — vendors the projection verb, records the module in the manifest, and creates `sources/`. Adoption of a wiki that predates Eddic adds two judgment-heavy steps: migrating player-safe static assets while rewriting site-rooted links to page-relative ones, and marking visibility page by page with the owner, failing closed so anything not verified as seen in play stays DM-only. The default taxonomy is the stock category set (characters, places, story, concepts, eras, species, sessions, systems), trimmed or extended only when a campaign clearly needs it and logged as a `schema` change. Twin pages are created only for topics that actually reveal progressively; empty twins are never pre-created. The verify harness projects a planted campaign and confirms that player pages and safe assets arrive, DM pages and `.dm` assets are withheld, twins behave, and a planted breach refuses the whole projection without writing.
